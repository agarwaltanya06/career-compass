# Finding Internships and Jobs

Not knowing *where* to look is the biggest hurdle. Here's where to search, how to apply, and how to avoid scams.

**Which fits you now?**
- **In school (Class 9–12):** start a simple CV and a free LinkedIn profile. Some internships are open to you.
- **Finished Class 12 / ITI / diploma / graduation:** all the options below are open, including government schemes.

---

## Where to look

**Internships & first jobs**
- [Internshala](https://internshala.com) — most popular in India for student internships + fresher jobs
- [LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com) — jobs + a free profile that works like an online CV
- [Naukri](https://www.naukri.com) — large job site (use the "fresher" filter)
- [Indeed](https://in.indeed.com) — search many companies at once

**Free government options**
- [National Career Service](https://www.ncs.gov.in) — govt portal: jobs + free career counselling
- [PM Internship Scheme](https://pminternship.mca.gov.in) — paid internships at big companies (mostly after school/college). **Use only this official site.** Check it for current rules.
- [Apprenticeships — NAPS](https://www.apprenticeshipindia.gov.in) — "earn while you learn", good for ITI/diploma/degree

**Government jobs (sarkari naukri)**
- [National Career Service](https://www.ncs.gov.in) · [Employment News](https://www.employmentnews.gov.in)
- Exam bodies: [SSC](https://ssc.gov.in) (staff posts) · [RRB](https://www.rrbcdg.gov.in) (railways) · [IBPS](https://www.ibps.in) (banking) · your state PSC

## How to apply — steps

1. **Make a CV** (see the CV Templates page).
2. **Create accounts** — start with Internshala + LinkedIn. Fill the profile fully.
3. **Search with filters** — location, "fresher" / "internship", your field.
4. **Apply to many**, not just one. No reply ≠ failure. It's a numbers game.
5. **Add a short message** if allowed — 2–3 honest lines on why you're interested.
6. **Track** where and when you applied.
7. **Prepare for the interview** (see Interview Preparation).

---

## ⚠️ Staying safe from scams

Fake "jobs" target young first-time seekers. These rules stop almost all of them:

- **Never pay to get a job.** Any "registration fee", "deposit", or "₹X for a guaranteed job" = scam. Real employers pay *you*. Government schemes are free.
- **Never share** your bank password, OTP, or full bank details.
- **"Too good to be true" = fake** — huge pay for little work, instant offers with no interview.
- **Check the address** — real government sites end in `.gov.in` or `.nic.in`. Type it yourself; don't trust forwarded links.
- **Unsure?** Slow down, ask a teacher or trusted adult. A real opportunity will still be there tomorrow.
